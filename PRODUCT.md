# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Global citizens managing money across borders — professionals, expats, and frequent travelers who hold accounts in multiple currencies. They're financially literate, design-aware, and accustomed to premium products in other categories (travel, hospitality, consumer tech). They use Banyan on mobile, primarily in quiet moments: checking balances, asking questions, approving payments.

## Product Purpose

Banyan is a premium consumer banking app for people whose financial lives span multiple countries. It surfaces what matters (balance, upcoming bills, recent activity), enables quick payments, and provides an AI layer that makes complex cross-border money management feel effortless. Success looks like a user who opens the app and immediately knows where they stand — globally.

## Brand Personality

Modern, personal, global. The brand is quietly assured — not loud, not corporate. It feels like a product designed by people who actually travel and live internationally. Warm without being casual. Intelligent without being cold.

## Anti-references

- Chase / Bank of America: stiff, legacy-bank density, zero personality, form-over-feeling
- Robinhood / crypto apps: gamified, neon, anxiety-inducing
- Generic fintech SaaS: navy + gold glassmorphism, hero metrics, identical card grids

## Design Principles

1. **Context-first**: surfaces the right information without the user having to ask. The app knows where you are, what's due, what's unusual.
2. **Earn trust through restraint**: premium doesn't mean dense or flashy. White space, weight contrast, and quiet motion communicate confidence.
3. **Global without flags**: the multi-currency reality is embedded in the UI naturally, not announced with icons and labels.
4. **AI as concierge, not chatbot**: the agent experience should feel like a knowledgeable person who knows your accounts, not a form with NLP.
5. **Motion serves orientation**: animations communicate state changes and spatial relationships, not delight for its own sake.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion support required on all animations. Sufficient contrast on all text — including placeholder text and secondary labels. Touch targets minimum 44×44px.
